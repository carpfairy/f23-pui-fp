Rayon, a semi-synthetic fiber, is created by combining wood pulp with synthetics. It is soft, breathable, and takes dyes easily. It drapes well and is often used as a silk alternative. However, rayon can shrink and wrinkle easily. It is also less durable, and may look cheap with wear.
