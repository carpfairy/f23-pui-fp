Cashmere is sourced primarily from the Himalayan region. Since it is a natural fiber, it is biodegradable. It is made from the undercoats of goats, which has led to overgrazing, land degradation. It can take up to four goats to produce the cashmere for one sweater. Furthermore, the RSPCA, an international animal welfare group, has raised concerns about the treatment of these goat farms.
