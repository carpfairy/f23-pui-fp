It can take up to 4 goats to produce the cashmere needed for one sweater, meaning that poor goat farming practices can lead to land degredation. Cashmere is mainly sourced from the Himalayan region, so international distribution contributes to carbon emissions. Virgin cashmere has an environmental impact roughly 100 times that of wool.
<br>
<div class="results-ethics"><b> Ethical Concerns:</b>
While goats naturally shed their hairs, the demand for cashmere far exceeds what they naturally produce. To collect hair faster, goats are brushed with metal combs. This is often a stressful and painful process. The RSPCA, an international animal welfare group, has raised concerns about the treatment of goats at cashmere farms.
