Linen is a natural fiber produced from the fibers of the flax plant, meaning it is biodegradable. It is one of the most sustainable fabrics in the industry. Linen is one of the oldest fibers known, dating back at least 36,000 years. It is renewable, since it is made from the flax plant. Flax cultivation requires minimal water and pesticides compared to cotton. Furthermore, the entire flax plant is used, minimizing waste.
